I thought I would give you guys a small review of the game I have been playing lately. It's called
Risk Your Life:2 and was made by a development team in Korea. The game itself is very similar to
Shadowbane. There are many different versions of this game floating around. Some versions are P2P
while others are F2P. The free version is located in Malaysia so, naturally, the people you will find
there are from that region. I played on the Malaysia server because I'm cheap.

So what's the deal, is it a good game or not? Well, the game itself I found to be fun but the problem
is with the development team and the people you play with. The dev team obviously can't program worth a damn
because the game has had a massive memory leak for months and they haven't bothered to try and patch it.
You can play for about 15 mins, depending on how much ram you have, until your system comes to a halt.
In addition to the leak there are numerous other server errors, crash to desktop and quest bugs to keep
you entertained while you wait for your virtually memory to kick in.

Now, the people, at least in my experience, were another major issue with the game. Never have I ever
run into more rude, obnoxious and just flat out mean people in an online game before. Just to give you a
perspective, I played Ultima Online for several years during the Dread Lord days. So, I've seen some
major pricks assholes jerks before.

The interesting aspect of this game is how they handle equipment. It's the same as most every other game in
the sense that you find various items of various magical properties, ect. The difference is that you
can "enhance" your item's stats an unlimited number of times by using resources
found in game. It's a real pain in the ass getting together enough resources to upgrade your items so what
the company has done is allowed people to buy gold and in game resources with real money. The effect this has on the
game is that all it takes to be stronger/better than someone else relies only on how much real money
you're willing to spend.
