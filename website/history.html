Our History

From a mercenary guild in the Ultima Online beta to one of the most feared names in online gaming.

1996 — The Beginning

Sosarian Armed Services

In the beginning there was Rivan and Aries. They played Warcraft, Red Alert, and Diablo together on the Kali network before securing spots in the Closed Beta of Ultima Online on the Pacific shard.

They formally established the Sosarian Armed Services — a mercenary guild selling their services where they were needed. The name was drawn from the game lore of the Ultima series. But Rivan and Aries quickly discovered something: you could amass far more wealth and reputation through killing other players. That discovery would dictate the guild's playstyle for the next three decades.

1997 — Sonoma Launch

Moving to Sonoma

When the Sonoma shard went live on December 13, 1997, SAS was there on day one. The guild immediately established itself as a Player Killer (PK) guild. A common theme among members was putting "of SAS" at the end of their character names — Rivan of SAS, Aries of SAS could be seen running through Sonoma in the very early days, terrorizing everyone around them.

Since Sonoma was a new shard and SAS already had a year of experience working together, they quickly became a dominant force. SHADOW purchased and placed his tower in the South Trinsic swamp during the first three months of Sonoma. Over time, this tower became the home and guild house of SAS on the Sonoma shard.

1998 — Growth & War

The Roster Grows

By mid-1998, membership had blossomed to 40+ players with notable additions including ChaoS, ArKaiNe, MoneyMaker, True Pimp, MasterMind, Psycho the Ripper, Drizzt Do-Urden, DragonMaster Dyn, Rainfall, and SHADOW.

The earliest clan war SAS fought was against the Chaotic Horde, led by Jeitarium — a group of Player Killers based in the Southern Trinsic jungles with a tower near the spawn point.

Another rivalry formed with the Lords of Evil (LoE), beginning when Rivan used a gate-travel bug to break into LoE leader Charger's house and steal a Chaos Shield.

In the spring of 1998, Origin released the infamous Rep Patch which implemented Stat Loss for "Murderers" — fundamentally changing the PK landscape of the game.

1998-1999 — Fight Nights

The Free City of Oasis

The Free City of Oasis was a player-run city formed by Jonas, Flaeme, Lady Rei, and Smitten w/Love. The city sponsored gladiatorial events called "Fight Nights" held on Sunday evenings, where hundreds of players would come to watch fighters battle it out inside GM-made cages.

Rivan started climbing the ranks at the Oasis Fight Nights, first as a contender and then as a "terrorist." At the end of the first year, Oasis celebrated by putting together the 50th Fight Night Tournament of Champions, inviting back the winners of the first 49 Fight Nights. Rivan was the victor.

1999 — Siege Perilous

A New Frontier

OSI launched a test server called Siege Perilous, hoping the power-gamers would consolidate there and leave the general population alone. SAS recruited many of their members from the Sonoma shard and logged in on the first day.

SAS started fast — grinding gold to secure a boat, traveling to Deceit where they farmed a large amount of gold, and ultimately securing a house and a guild stone. However, the server was wiped in the days after, and with it all of SAS's possessions.

The members who stayed on Siege Perilous formed a new guild: Disciples of the Moment (DTM), led by Meursalt and the late Orin Warrick.

1999-2000 — The Great War

BLD vs SAS

SAS members who returned from Siege Perilous to Sonoma ignited what became the defining conflict of the shard — the BLD vs SAS War. What started as skirmishes between the guild and the Blood Clan (BLD) escalated into a server-wide conflict between the PKs and the Anti-PKs.

A huge rivalry emerged between Rivan of SAS, MRGrimm of SAS and the BLD founders Grunk Half Orc and rukNar the Orc. As the war raged on, both sides drew in allies, and the battles became bigger and bigger. This war lasted for months.

There was no clear winner — just a lot of bloodied and battered participants. Out of this war came a great amount of respect from both sides. Many formed friendships and bonds between the two guilds, even as they remained enemies on the field.

2000 — A New Name

Sinners Among Saints

Circa 2000, NeXuS renamed the guild from Sosarian Armed Services to Sinners Among Saints. The guild would continue to transform, becoming one of the most feared names in UO history. Their playstyle had an impact on how Ultima's designers crafted the game post-launch and helped shape modern MMORPG game design.

2001-2004 — Later UO Years

Age of Shadows & Beyond

SAS continued to be active on Sonoma through the Age of Shadows expansion and various changes to the game. Members also played on other shards including Chesapeake, and the guild maintained its reputation as a competitive force throughout this era.

2004+ — New Games

Beyond Ultima Online

The early years of the guild, 1996 through 2004, were strongly concentrated in Ultima Online. Over the following decades, SAS fanned out into communities across many different games: Counter-Strike, Asheron's Call, World of Warcraft, Lineage, and many more.

In World of Warcraft, SAS established themselves as a Horde guild on the Kil'Jaeden US PvP server. The competitive spirit that was born on the fields of Sonoma carried forward into every new world they entered.

The group evolved to have members of all walks of life — climatologists, lawyers, game developers, entrepreneurs — spread across North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia. What began as a ragtag band of PKs became a lifelong brotherhood.

2005-2010 — Free Shards

Returning to Britannia

SAS members reunited on various UO free shards over the years, including Redemption, IPY (In Por Ylem), UO Hybrid, and others. These servers offered a return to the classic gameplay that had defined SAS's early years, and old rivalries and friendships were rekindled.

Present

Still Standing

Three decades later, the Sinners Among Saints endure. The guild continues as a community of close friends bound together by shared history — from the swamps of Trinsic to whatever comes next.
